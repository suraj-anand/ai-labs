### What is an LLM?
- A **Large Language Model (LLM)** is an **Artificial Intelligence (AI)** model that is trained on a very large amount of **text data**.  
- It learns patterns, grammar, facts, and relationships in language from this data.  
- Because of this training, an LLM can **understand natural language input** and **generate natural language text as a response**.  


### What is GPT?

- **GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer)** is a type of **Large Language Model (LLM)**
- It is based on the **Transformer neural network architecture**, which is designed to process and generate natural language.  
- GPT models are commonly used for tasks such as **conversation, text generation, summarization, coding assistance, and question answering**.


### What is a Transformer?

- A **Transformer** is a type of **neural network architecture** used in **Natural Language Processing (NLP)** and many modern **Artificial Intelligence (AI)** systems.  
- It was introduced in the research paper **"Attention Is All You Need" (2017)** by researchers at Google Brain.

- The main idea of the Transformer is the **attention mechanism**, especially **self-attention**, which allows the model to understand the relationships between words in a sentence.

- Instead of processing text **word-by-word in sequence** like older models (such as **Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs)**), Transformers process **all words in parallel**.  
  This makes training **much faster and more efficient**.

- A Transformer model typically has two main parts:
  - **Encoder** – processes and understands the input text.
  - **Decoder** – generates the output text.

- Transformers are the foundation of many modern AI models, including **BERT**, **GPT**, and other **Large Language Models (LLMs)**.

- Because of their ability to capture **long-range relationships in text**, Transformers perform very well on tasks such as **translation, summarization, question answering, and text generation**.
